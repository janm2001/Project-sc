Stephen Curry

Born in Ohio in 1988 to former NBA player Dell Curry, Stephen Curry garnered national attention for his impressive play at Davidson College. He was drafted in 2009 by the Golden State Warriors, and eventually developed into one of pro basketball's top players with his stellar shooting skills. After garnering Most Valuable Player honors and helping the Warriors win the NBA championship in 2015, Curry led the team to a league-record 73 wins the following season. In May 2016, Curry became the first person to be named Most Valuable Player by unanimous vote in NBA history, and one of only 11 players to win the MVP award two years in a row. In 2017, he helped lead the Warriors to another NBA championship, defeating the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Latest News about Stephen Curry


Stephen Curry vs Clipers

Stephen Curry hit 9-of-14 shots, 7-of-11 3-pointers and 6-of-8 free throws for 31 points, five rebounds, six dimes, two steals and a block in Monday's easy 141-113 win over the Clippers. As if that weren't enough, Curry also produced just one turnover on the night. The 31 points marked his second-highest scoring game of the season and the seven 3-pointers were a season high. The Warriors have overcome a slow start to win four of their last five games.


Stephen Curry vs Pistons

Stephen Curry scored 27 points on 11-of-17 shooting with eight assists, six rebounds, one steal and three 3-pointers on Sunday, but he also had five of the Warriors' 25 turnovers. Before the game, Pistons coach Stan Van Gundy called the Warriors "the hardest team to guard in the history of the NBA." He looked prophetic in the first quarter, as Golden State jumped out to a 12-point lead, but the Warriors were their own worst enemy with a variety of miscues down the stretch tonight. Steph faces another challenge vs. Pat Beverley and the Clippers on Monday.


Stephen Curry vs Raptors

Stephen Curry led the Warriors to a late comeback on Wednesday, scoring 30 points with five assists, four rebounds, three steals and one block in a win vs. the Raptors. Curry hit 9-of-20 field goals (4-of-11 from deep) and 8-of-8 FTs tonight. That gives him an incredible 51 consecutive free throw makes, so he's only 46 behind NBA record-holder Micheal Williams, who sank 97 straight with the Wolves in 1993. For future reference, Steph loves playing the Raptors -- he came in averaging 30.2 points and 8.2 assists in 14 career appearances against them, his highest averages vs. any team.
